Pronunciation Tips:

  • Greek is a phonetic language - words are pronounced as they're written
  • Accent marks (΄) indicate which syllable to stress
  • Double consonants are pronounced separately
  • Some letter combinations create unique sounds (e.g., μπ = b, ντ = d)

I really enjoy creating beautiful websites about tourism and travel.

Greek Culture & Idioms

Understanding Greek culture and idioms is essential for mastering the language and connecting with Greek speakers.

Greek Cultural Etiquette

  • Philoxenia: The famous Greek hospitality - Greeks are known for their warm welcome to strangers
  • Family Values: Family is central to Greek life, with strong intergenerational bonds
  • Gesture Communication: Greeks often use hand gestures to emphasize speech
  • Mealtime: Lunch is typically the main meal, eaten around 2-3 PM
  • Name Days: Celebrating one's name day (day of the saint they're named after) is often more important than birthdays

Common Greek Idioms

In English: "It's all Greek to me"

In Greek: "Αυτά μου φαίνονται κινέζικα" (These seem Chinese to me)

Meaning: Something is completely incomprehensible

In English: "To put oil on the fire"

In Greek: "Βάζω λάδι στη φωτιά" (I put oil on the fire)

Meaning: To make a bad situation worse

In English: "To have ants in your pants"

In Greek: "Έχω τσουτσέκια" (I have little ants)

Meaning: To be restless or unable to sit still

In English: "When the pig flies"

In Greek: "Όταν βγει ο ήλιος από τη δυσμή" (When the sun rises from the west)

Meaning: Something that will never happen

In English: "To break the egg in your pocket"

In Greek: "Σπάω το αυγό στην τσέπη μου" (I break the egg in my pocket)

Meaning: To spend all your money

Traditional Greek Celebrations

  • Name Days (Ονομαστική Εορτή): Celebrated with visits, phone calls, and often small gatherings
  • Easter (Πάσχα): The most important religious holiday with midnight church services and festive meals
  • Apokries (Carnival): A period of celebration before Lent with costumes and parties
  • August 15th (Κοίμηση της Θεοτόκου): A major religious holiday honoring the Virgin Mary
  • Ochi Day (Επέτειος του Όχι): October 28th, commemorating Greece's refusal to surrender to Italy in WWII

Greek Superstitions

  • Matiasma (Evil Eye): The belief that someone can harm you with an envious glance
  • Spitting for Good Luck: Greeks might pretend to spit three times to ward off bad luck
  • Tuesday the 13th: Considered unlucky (not Friday the 13th)
  • Knocking on Wood: To avoid tempting fate after mentioning something positive
  • Pomegranate: Smashing a pomegranate on New Year's Day brings good luck

Greek for Work

Τι κάνει ένας Front-End Developer;

Ο Front-End Developer είναι υπεύθυνος για τη σχεδίαση και την υλοποίηση του UI (User Interface) ενός website ή web application. Δημιουργεί το κομμάτι με το οποίο αλληλεπιδρούν οι χρήστες, δίνοντας έμφαση τόσο στην αισθητική όσο και στη λειτουργικότητα.

What does a Front-End Developer do?

The Front-End Developer is responsible for designing and implementing the UI (User Interface) of a website or web application. They create the part with which users interact, emphasizing both aesthetics and functionality.

Βασικές τεχνολογίες που χρησιμοποιεί ένας Front-End Developer:

- HTML, CSS – Δομή και στυλ των ιστοσελίδων
- JavaScript – Διαδραστικότητα και δυναμικότητα
- Frameworks & Libraries – React.js, Vue.js, Angular
- UI/UX Design Principles – Κατανόηση καλών πρακτικών σχεδίασης

Basic technologies used by a Front-End Developer:

- HTML, CSS – Structure and style of websites
- JavaScript – Interactivity and dynamism
- Frameworks & Libraries – React.js, Vue.js, Angular
- UI/UX Design Principles – Understanding good design practices
